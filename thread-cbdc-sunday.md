# Thread CBDC - Retail State of Play
## To be published Sunday 8 AM

---

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
134 countries are exploring CBDCs.
Only 3 have a functioning retail system.
And even then, nobody really uses them.

Here's why the digital money revolution is stalling 🧵

---

**Tweet 2 (The Paradox)**
The promise: instant payments, no intermediaries, for everyone.

The reality:
• China: 260M wallets, but only 0.16% of money in circulation
• Nigeria: 13M wallets created, 98.5% inactive
• Bahamas (world's first CBDC): 30% of population enrolled, usage near zero

Where's the problem?

---

**Tweet 3 (The China Case)**
China is the only "success" in volume.

But their secret isn't technological:
→ Forced integration with WeChat Pay and Alipay (1.5 billion users)
→ Distribution of digital "red packets" (stimulus)
→ Testing since 2020, still in pilot phase

Without an existing ecosystem, even the Chinese state struggles to impose its currency.

---

**Tweet 4 (The Instructive Failures)**
Nigeria launched Africa's first CBDC in 2021.
Result after 3 years:
• 0.5% of population uses it regularly
• Commercial banks boycott it
• Citizens prefer cash and stablecoins

Why? Digital infrastructure was weak, trust too.

---

**Tweet 5 (The Caribbean Lesson)**
Bahamas and Jamaica: pioneers but trapped.

Their mistake: believing "digital = automatically better"

On-the-ground reality:
→ 80% of transactions remain cash
→ Merchants don't have adapted POS systems
→ Banks don't participate

A CBDC without a distribution network = an app nobody downloads.

---

**Tweet 6 (Does India Show the Way?)**
India has mature digital infrastructure (UPI: 20 billion transactions/month).

Yet their CBDC stagnates too:
• Only 6-7M users
• <0.003% of total money supply

Indians ask: "Why change? UPI already works, for free."

---

**Tweet 7 (What Actually Works)**
One exception: cases where CBDC does what others can't.

In India, Odisha state uses CBDC for PROGRAMMABLE social aid distribution:
→ Money can only be spent on certain categories
→ Automatic transfer if conditions met
→ 88,000 beneficiaries, zero fraud

Programmability is the real killer feature.

---

**Tweet 8 (The Brazil Model)**
Brazil doesn't copy. They innovate.

Their "Drex" isn't just digital cash:
→ Tokenized credit for SMEs
→ Fractional asset securitization
→ Smart contracts for automatic financing

$387M already tokenized in pilot phase.

They're building on Pix success (63 billion transactions/year).

---

**Tweet 9 (The Uncomfortable Truth)**
No retail CBDC has exceeded 0.5% of money in circulation.

This isn't a bug, it's reality:
• Cash remains king in the informal economy
• Existing cards and wallets are "good enough"
• CBDCs don't bring enough added value... yet

---

**Tweet 10 (The Future)**
CBDCs won't replace your daily payments tomorrow.

But they could become essential for:
• Targeted social transfers
• Programmable international trade
• Financial inclusion where banks don't go

The experimentation time isn't over. Europe decides in 2026.
