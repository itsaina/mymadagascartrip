---

**Tweet 2 (The Problem)**
David Sinclair's team at Harvard Medical School asked:

Why can't adult mammals regenerate nerves?

A salamander can regrow an eye.
A mouse cannot.

The difference isn't DNA — it's how it's read.

---

**Tweet 3 (The Clock)**
Every cell has the same DNA.
But a brain cell reads different genes than a skin cell.

This is the epigenome — chemical switches that turn genes on/off.

Sinclair's theory: Aging is loss of these switching patterns.
Cells "forget" who they are.

---

**Tweet 4 (Yamanaka Factors)**
The solution: Three genes normally active in embryos.

Oct4, Sox2, Klf4 — called "Yamanaka factors."

Together they can reprogram any cell back to stem cell state.

But full reprogramming causes cancer.
Sinclair used them partially.

---

**Tweet 5 (The Method)**
Delivery: Modified virus (AAV) injected into mouse retinas.

Target: Damaged optic nerve cells.

Result:
→ 2x more surviving nerve cells
→ 5x more nerve regrowth
→ Vision restored after complete loss

---

**Tweet 6 (Glaucoma Reversed)**
Glaucoma = leading cause of blindness worldwide.

It destroys the optic nerve. Irreversibly. Until now.

Sinclair's treated mice regained visual acuity.

"Regaining visual function after injury has rarely been demonstrated."

---

**Tweet 7 (Aging Reversed)**
The ultimate test: 12-month-old mice (elderly for mice).

Treated retinas showed:
→ Gene patterns of young mice
→ Electrical signals restored
→ Vision recovered

The clock doesn't just measure time. It IS time.

---

**Tweet 8 (Mechanism)**
DNA methylation = chemical tags on DNA that change with age.

Young cells have specific patterns.
Old cells lose them.

Treatment reversed these patterns.

Proof: Methylation isn't just a marker of aging. It's a driver.

---

**Tweet 9 (Safety)**
The fear: Reprogramming cells = cancer risk.

But partial reprogramming avoided teratomas.

No tumors in treated mice.
Vision restored without side effects.

The method appears safe — so far.
